# Bothan Jura Retreat — Design System Brief (v1)

This document defines the foundational visual and experiential design principles for the Bothan Jura Retreat rebuild. It draws directly from the official brand brief, Kieran/Makgill/Shallish materials, and our agreed direction.

This is the **core design brief** that will guide:
- Figma Design System v1
- AI scaffold layouts in Wix Studio
- Component creation
- Page templates
- Future long‑tail modules (Guides, Book Club, Archive)

---

# 1. Brand Aesthetic

The Bothan Jura aesthetic is:
- **Minimal**
- **Editorial**
- **Poetic**
- **White‑space led**
- **Typographically expressive**
- **Calm and contemplative**
- **Inspired by literary journals, galleries, and art books**
- **Rooted in Jura’s wild landscape**
- **Authentic, natural, atmospheric**

Avoid clichés:
- Not glossy or corporate
- Not generic “cabin / hip hut” style
- Not high‑energy or pattern-heavy
- Not bright or saturated
- Not gimmicky or over‑animated

The design must feel:
- Slow
- Intimate
- Atmospheric
- Quietly luxurious
- Intelligent
- Bookish

# 2. Typography System

## **Primary Header Typeface**
**The Seasons**  
- Used for H1–H3
- Literary, bold, sculptural
- Sets the poetic, editorial tone
- Works beautifully large with generous whitespace

## **Body Typeface**
**IBM Plex Mono**  
- Modern, clean, technical-but-human
- Reinforces the “catalogue / archive / log book” undertone
- Used for body text, captions, metadata, UI labels

## **Typographic Behaviour**
- Large point sizes for H1/H2
- Generous line height for readability
- Body text short line lengths (ideal 60–75 characters)
- Pull quotes styled as oversized serif or mono blocks
- Section labels in mono uppercase
- Tight, intentional scale (defined in DS v1)

## **Typography Pairing Logic**
- Serif = emotional, atmospheric, narrative
- Mono = factual, structured, calm, precise

# 4. Spacing & Layout System

The site must have a **calm rhythm**, not crowded or dense.

### **Spacing Philosophy**
- Breathing space is part of the brand
- Large negative space between sections
- Comfortable padding around text blocks
- Avoid walls of text or compact grids

### **Spacing Tokens (draft)**
(Refined in DS v1 — approximate for brief)
- XS: 4
- S: 8
- M: 16
- L: 32
- XL: 48
- XXL: 80

### **Section Rhythm**
- Each section treated like a “page spread” in a journal
- Alternating wide image → narrow text → whitespace
- Editorial pacing: moments of breath

### **Grid System**
- Desktop: 12‑column grid, wide margins
- Tablet: 6–8 column collapse
- Mobile: 4‑column, centred
- No overly symmetrical “template-y” grids
- Use asymmetry where appropriate (Makgill influence)

# 5. Imagery & Art Direction

### **Image Tone**
- Natural light
- Mist, water, stone, wood
- Soft contrast
- Earthy, muted palette
- No oversaturation
- Quiet compositions

### **Image Roles**
- Used sparingly
- Large immersive hero images
- Full-width landscapes to create pace shifts
- Portraits / cabin images framed with generous margins

### **Cropping**
- Mix of framed and full-bleed depending on narrative moment
- Avoid over‑cropped or busy images

### **Texture Cues**
- Stone
- Weathered wood
- Sand
- Paper grain
- Corten steel

These support the brand’s tactile, authentic feel.

# 7. Navigation & Header

### **Header**
- Minimal, monochrome
- Serif logo or wordmark left
- Burger menu right
- Transparent over hero, solid on scroll

### **Menu (Overlay)**
- Full-screen overlay
- Large serif type for nav items
- Mono for sublabels
- High whitespace
- Calm transitions (fade only)

# 8. Voice & Copy Guidelines

The Bothan Jura tone is:
- Bookish
- Poetic
- Intelligent
- Calm
- Sensory
- Place-rooted
- Understated, never salesy

# 9. Design Style Summary (Core Identity)

The design should feel like:
- A small independent publishing house
- A gallery catalogue
- A poetic field notebook
- A calm, intelligent space

Characteristics:
- Big type, small details
- Generous whitespace
- Minimal colour
- Strong grid discipline
- Slow pacing / breathing room
- Tactile, natural image selection

# 9\.1 Design References (Visual & Conceptual)

These references define the *feel* and *design language* we aspire to. They should be used for art direction, layout thinking, typography, whitespace, and editorial pacing.

## **Publishing Houses / Books (Primary Influence)**
- **Phaidon** — high-end art publishing, rigorous grids, large imagery, elegant restraint
- **Taschen** — luxurious book layouts, generous white space, bold chapter openers
- **Fitzcarraldo Editions** — minimalist, literary, typographically driven, strong serif identity
- **New Directions** — poetic, spacious, quiet, cult literary aesthetic
- **Faber & Faber (classic editions)** — typographic confidence, modernist influence

These guide:
- typography hierarchy
- whitespace discipline
- pacing between sections
- the "gallery or monograph" presentation of content

## **Digital References (Structure & Behaviour)**
- **Brinkerhoff Poetry Foundation** — framed sections, page-like pacing, white space
- **Steuermann Haus** — narrative feel, east–west navigation, editorial visuals
- **José Saramago Foundation** — slow, elegant reading experience, quiet design
- **Ableton “Making Music”** — clean structures, minimalistic UI, unobtrusive navigation
- **Sally Bliumis-Dunn** — big typography entry, simple structure
- **Brandpad.io** — clean loading, bold typographic discipline
- **Fitzcarraldo Editions (website aesthetic)** — literary minimalism

These guide:
- layout patterns
- menu behaviour
- rhythm
- how imagery interacts with content
- how text dominates the emotional tone

## **Positioning References (Conceptual)**
- **Art gallery catalogues**
- **Independent literary journals**
- **Architectural portfolios**
- **Field notebooks / log books** (Studio Makgill influence)

These inform:
- the "calm intelligence" tone
- the catalogue/archival undertone
- how Bothans and Guides are treated as curated objects
